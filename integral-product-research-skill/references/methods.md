# Methods Reference

Use this reference for concise method structures. Keep outputs practical and evidence-aware.

## AJTBD / JTBD structure

Analyze a segment through the progress a customer is trying to make in a concrete context.

Fields:

- Situation/context: when the job appears.
- Trigger: what starts active search or dissatisfaction.
- Main Job: the core progress sought.
- Functional Jobs: tasks the customer must complete.
- Emotional Jobs: feelings the customer wants to achieve or avoid.
- Social Jobs: how the customer wants to be perceived.
- Related Jobs: adjacent progress needs before/after the main job.
- Desired Outcomes: measurable improvements customers would value.
- Current workaround: what they do today.
- Forces: push of current pain, pull of new solution, anxiety of switching, habit/inertia.
- Evidence status: fact, hypothesis, assumption.

## ABCDX segmentation lens

Use ABCDX as a practical segment description checklist:

- A — Audience/Actors: who experiences the job, who pays, who influences, who blocks.
- B — Behavior: current behavior, frequency, workflow, current alternatives.
- C — Context: industry, geography, regulation, urgency, technology environment.
- D — Desired outcome/Demand: progress desired, willingness-to-pay hypothesis, success metric.
- X — eXecution constraints: reachability, channel access, integration needs, procurement, risk, budget, timing.

## RAT — Riskiest Assumption Test

Rank assumptions by:

- Impact if false: 1 low, 5 existential.
- Uncertainty: 1 well-evidenced, 5 unknown.
- Testability: 1 hard/slow, 5 easy/fast.
- RAT priority = Impact × Uncertainty × Testability.

Prioritize tests that can disprove fatal assumptions quickly and cheaply.

## Customer Development plan

Include:

- Learning goals.
- Interview segments and recruiting channels.
- Screening criteria.
- Interview guide by topic, not leading questions.
- Sample size recommendation by segment.
- Consent/anonymization note.
- Note-taking schema.
- Synthesis plan.
- Decision rules.

Good question patterns:

- “Расскажите о последнем случае, когда…”
- “Что вы сделали вместо этого?”
- “Сколько времени/денег это заняло?”
- “Что было самым сложным?”
- “Какие решения вы пробовали и почему отказались?”

Avoid leading questions such as “Would you use our product?” unless paired with commitment tests.

## Validation experiment menu

Use the cheapest credible test for each risk:

- Problem interview.
- Solution interview with prototype.
- Concierge test.
- Wizard-of-Oz test.
- Smoke-test landing page.
- Fake-door test.
- Paid pilot / LOI / pre-order.
- Pricing test.
- Manual workflow simulation.
- Prototype usability test.
- Data feasibility spike.
- Technical proof-of-concept.
- Channel test.

## Prioritization scales

Use 1–5 scales and define them. Do not hide uncertainty behind exact-looking scores. Mark scores as hypotheses unless evidence-backed.
